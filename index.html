Three Health Tips for Students Headed Back to School

By: John Honan

Published: October 4, 2020

Maintain a Healthy Sleep Schedule

When the world seemingly stopped back in March, sleep schedules were distrupted. Children stayed up all night on their phones knowing that school would be a short walk from their bed. Now that students are returnng to school buildings, getting back on a routine sleep schedule is important. Avoiding electronics before bed can help students get to sleep at a reasonable time depedning on when classes According to Science Daily, many people get sick when they are not getting enough sleep. A 2017 study shows that people who don't get enough sleep have a depressed immune system.

Source: Science Daily

Pack All the Supplies You Need the Night Before

Take time each night to pack your bag with everything you need for the school day. Remember, sharing pens and pencils will not be an option this school year. Therefore, it's more important than ever to come to class prepared. Keep a bag packed with extra supplies so that you will never be caught short. COVID-19 can easily be spread from sharing supplies with other students.

Pack A Healthy Lunch To Boost Immune System

Maintaining a healthy diet is an essential part of keeping your immune system strong enough to fight off viruses. Packing fruits and vegetables, instead of chips and salty foods, can help boost your immune system. A diet that lacks balances nutrients and vitamins can impair the production of immune cells in the body. This makes it harder for your body to fight off infections, such as COVID-19. It also makes it easier for you to catch a virus from someone else.
